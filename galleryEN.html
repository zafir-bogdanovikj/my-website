A small gallery of my travels

ITALY

Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II

Is a 19th-century historic arcade connecting the Duomo and La Scala. Today it stands as a symbol of elegance, with luxury shops, cafés, and striking architecture.

BAM – Biblioteca degli Alberi Milano

Is a contemporary urban park in Porta Nuova, known for its unique design and open event space. It blends nature, culture, and daily life in Milan.

Duomo di Milano

Cathedral of stunning Gothic architecture and the most iconic symbol of Milan. Its rooftop offers one of the best panoramic views of the city.

HUNGARY

Hungarian Parliament

One of the most striking landmarks in Budapest, set along the Danube. With its Neo-Gothic architecture, it stands as both a political and visual symbol of the city.

Buda Castle

Historic palace of Hungarian kings, perched on a hill above the Danube. Today it houses museums and art, offering panoramic views of all of Budapest.

Central Market Hall

The largest and most iconic market in Budapest, housed in a vibrant 19th-century building. It’s where local food, souvenirs, and tradition all come together.

NETHERLANDS

Scheveningen

Is a seaside district of The Hague, known for its long sandy beaches, pier, and promenade. It’s a popular destination for swimming, strolling, and enjoying the summer vibe by the North Sea.

Herengracht

The iconic narrow buildings along Amsterdam’s canals are a hallmark of the city. The calm water flowing beside them reveals the historic layout and charm of Dutch architecture.

Stadhuis Delft

Is a 17th-century historic town hall located on the main square of Delft. It's known for its Renaissance facade and its role in the city’s public and political life.

GERMANY

Ehemaliges Koniglich & Festplatz Elbe

Part of Dresden’s cultural and historical core. They combine architectural grandeur with open space for public events and festivals.

Schlossplatz

Central square in Dresden’s historic Old Town, surrounded by landmarks like the Royal Palace and the Cathedral. It’s a perfect starting point for exploring the city’s rich history.

Cologne Cathedral

One of the largest Gothic cathedrals in Europe and Germany’s most iconic religious building. With its towering spires and impressive architecture, it dominates the skyline of Cologne.

SERBIA

Mokra Gora

Is a scenic village in western Serbia, known for the Šargan Eight railway and the ethno-village Drvengrad. It’s a place where nature, culture, and tradition come together.

Kalemegdan

Belgrade’s most prominent historical fortress, located at the confluence of the Danube and Sava rivers. Today, it serves as a park, museum, and scenic viewpoint connecting history with daily life.

Zlatibor Lake

With its iconic central fountain, is one of Zlatibor’s recognizable landmarks. It’s a perfect spot for a walk, a photo, or a quiet break by the water.
